# Code of Conduct

This project is built by a community of people with different backgrounds,
experience levels, and goals. These guidelines exist so that everyone can take
part productively and feel welcome doing so. They are written specifically for
warmbly-go and are not derived from any third-party document.

## What we expect

- **Be respectful.** Assume good faith. Disagree with ideas, not with people.
- **Be constructive.** Critique code and proposals with reasons and, where you
  can, suggestions. "This is wrong" helps no one; "this breaks X because Y"
  does.
- **Be patient and welcoming.** Many contributors are new to Go, to open
  source, or to this codebase. Questions are how people learn.
- **Be considerate.** Your work depends on the work of others, and others will
  depend on yours. Keep that in mind in reviews, issues, and discussions.

## What is not acceptable

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind.
- Personal attacks, demeaning comments, or sustained disruption of discussion.
- Publishing other people's private information without their explicit consent.
- Unwelcome sexual attention or imagery.

These apply in all project spaces — issues, pull requests, discussions, code
review, and any official communication channel — and when an individual is
representing the project in public.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates these guidelines, please
report it privately to **conduct@warmbly.com**. Reports are handled
confidentially. Include what happened, where, and any context that helps us
understand the situation; links or screenshots are useful when available.

We will acknowledge a report within a few business days and follow up with the
people involved.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for upholding these guidelines and may take any
action they consider appropriate and proportionate, including editing or
removing contributions, issuing a warning, or temporarily or permanently
restricting a person's participation in the project. Maintainers who do not
follow these guidelines in good faith may face consequences decided by other
members of the project's leadership.

We aim to be fair: where practical, we will explain the reason for a moderation
decision to the person affected.

## Scope of this document

This Code of Conduct covers conduct within the project. Security issues should
be reported as described in [SECURITY.md](./SECURITY.md), and general support
questions belong in the channels listed in [SUPPORT.md](./SUPPORT.md).
